# MyFood - Comprehensive AI Culinary Assistant

## 1. Project Overview
**MyFood** is a mobile application designed to function as a comprehensive culinary assistant. It solves the daily struggles of meal planning, budget management, and actively combats food waste (Zero Waste). Through an AI-powered chat, users can generate weekly meal plans, create optimized shopping lists with real-time price comparisons, and receive step-by-step cooking assistance.

**Founders:** Rodrigo Huajamaita, Lucca, and Vicenzo Giordana.
**Location:** Libertador San Martín, Entre Ríos, Argentina.
**Target Audience:** Urban professionals (ages 25-45) and young, dual-income families looking to optimize their time and household budget.

## 2. Technical Architecture (Stack)
For all code generated in this repository, the following technological guidelines must be strictly followed:

*   **Frontend:** React Native (iOS & Android).
*   **Backend:** Elixir with the Phoenix framework (API-only mode, `--no-html`, `--no-assets`).
*   **Database:** PostgreSQL (managed via Ecto).
*   **Real-Time Communication:** Phoenix Channels / WebSockets (crucial for streaming AI chat responses).
*   **Backend Design Pattern:** Clean Architecture. Strict separation of concerns between the Data Layer (Ecto/Repos), Business Logic (pure Phoenix Contexts), and Transport Layer (Controllers/Channels).
*   **Authentication:** JWT using the Guardian library.

## 3. Core Business Rules
When generating logic, adhere to the following domain rules:

1.  **Multi-tenancy (Group vs. Individual):** The system supports both individual and group (family) accounts. `MealPlans`, `Budgets`, and `ShoppingItems` belong to an `Account` (Household), not to an isolated user. All members within a group share the same visibility and data.
2.  **Budget Limits:** Every meal plan generated by the AI must be strictly constrained by the `Budget` configured for that specific `Account`.
3.  **Inventory Prioritization (Zero Waste):** The AI engine must prioritize ingredients that the user has already marked as available in their inventory to prevent expiration and waste.
4.  **Monetization (SaaS, in-app purchases):** Three paid tiers, all billed in **USD** through **RevenueCat** (Apple StoreKit + Google Play Billing). The app ships to **App Store + Play Store** (decided grill 2026-06-16). No permanent free tier — every new `Account` starts with a **14-day trial** that unlocks all features. After the trial, the `Account` is read-only until a plan is purchased.

    | Plan           | Price (USD/month) | Seats              | Notes                                              |
    | -------------- | ----------------- | ------------------ | -------------------------------------------------- |
    | `individual`   | $1.99             | 1 (owner only)     | Solo user. Default for new accounts.               |
    | `family_4`     | $2.99             | up to 4 (1 + 3)    | Owner + 3 invited members.                         |
    | `family_6`     | $3.99             | up to 6 (1 + 5)    | Owner + 5 invited members.                         |

    "Seats" is **"up to N"** (not "exactly N"): a `family_4` may have 2, 3, or 4 members. The seat count is enforced on **invite** and on **reactivation** of a suspended membership — not on the count of currently-active members.

    **Downgrade policy:** if the owner downgrades from a higher plan to a lower one and currently-active members exceed the lower cap, the downgrade is **blocked** until the owner removes members. The UI surfaces the required count and a "Remove N members" action. No silent suspension, no silent ejection.

    **Free trial (decided grill 2026-06-16):** every newly-registered `User` gets a **14-day `family_6` trial** that starts **automatically at registration**, no card required. The trial unlocks all features (full `family_6` access — even if the Account will later be `:individual`). After the trial expires without conversion, the `Account` becomes **`:read_only`** — the user can still view historical plans, inventory, and recipes but cannot create new plans, chat with the AI, or modify the shopping list. Persistent in-app nudges guide the upgrade. The trial is **bound to the `User`**, not to the Account: if the owner leaves and a new owner takes over, the new owner does not inherit the trial. Abuse mitigation: 1 trial per email + 1 trial per device (enforced server-side).

## 4. Core Domain Entities (Conceptual Schema)
*   **`Account`**: The primary entity that groups users. Defines the plan type (`:individual` or `:group`).
*   **`User`**: A person's identity and credentials (email, password, name, avatar). A `User` exists independently of any `Account` — they always have their own login.
*   **`Account`**: A container of shared resources. Has `type` (`:individual` | `:family`) and exactly one `owner_id` (a `User` who pays and administers). An `:individual` account has exactly one member (the owner); a `:family` account has 2-6 members. Every `MealPlan`, `Budget`, `ShoppingItem`, `Message`, and `InventoryItem` belongs to exactly one `Account`.
*   **`AccountMembership`**: The join entity between `User` and `Account`. Carries `role` (`:owner` | `:member`), `status` (`:active` | `:invited` | `:suspended`), `invited_by` (User), `joined_at`. A `User` can hold **multiple** `AccountMembership`s (own `:individual` + memberships in family accounts they were invited to). At any moment, exactly one is "active" for the session — the JWT carries `account_id` and `membership_id` claims. Switching accounts = re-issuing the JWT.
*   **Spotify analogy (grill 2026-06-16):** The model is "Spotify Family for meal plans" — each person keeps their own login, the owner pays, members are invited. The difference: resources (meal plans, shopping list, inventory) are **shared** within an account (Spotify keeps each user's library private; MyFood does not).
*   **Design decision (grill 2026-06-16):** Real `AccountMembership` is the canonical model. Current `meal_planner_api` code has `User.account_id` directly (no membership table, no `Account.owner_id`) — this is flagged as a **refactor-in-place** that the first `sdd-new` change for "multi-tenant families" must address. The first family-focused change should also define role permissions and the invite flow.
*   **`MealPlan`**: The weekly plan generated by the AI.
*   **`Meal`**: A specific dish assigned to a day and time (breakfast, lunch, etc.).
*   **`ShoppingItem`**: Required ingredients consolidated into a single list per `Account`.
*   **`Budget`**: The financial constraint associated with an `Account`.
*   **`PlanningSession`**: A **temporal, single-User** conversation between one `User` and the AI to produce one `MealPlan`. A `PlanningSession` has `status` (`:open` | `:confirmed` | `:expired` | `:abandoned`), `owner_user_id` (the User operating the chat), `opened_at`, `last_activity_at`, `confirmed_at` (nullable), and `expires_at` (`opened_at + 24h`). At any moment an `Account` has **at most one `:open` `PlanningSession`** — the system enforces this with a unique partial index. The `:open` session is also the **lock**: no other `User` of the same `Account` can open a new session while one is `:open`. There is **no take-over** (decided grill 2026-06-16).
*   **`PlanningMessage`**: A single message in a `PlanningSession`. Carries `author` (`:user` | `:assistant` | `:system`), `body` (text), `content_type` (`:text` | `:speech_transcript` | `:tool_call`), and `created_at`. **Scoped to the `PlanningSession` — not to the `Account`.** When a `PlanningSession` is `:confirmed`, its `PlanningMessage`s are **archived read-only for 30 days** (visible to all members, not editable, not extensible) and then hard-deleted. While `:open`, only the `owner_user_id` can read or write. After `:confirmed`/`:expired`/`:abandoned`, the messages are read-only for any `Account` member.
*   **Lifecycle (decided grill 2026-06-16):**
    - **Open** → any member starts a chat → `PlanningSession.status = :open`, `owner_user_id = <User>`, `expires_at = now + 24h`.
    - **Inactivity** → if no message for 24h → auto-transition to `:expired`. The `MealPlan` is **not** persisted.
    - **Confirmation** → the owner presses the check button → the proposed plan is persisted as a `MealPlan` and `PlanningSession.status = :confirmed`. The messages become read-only.
    - **Explicit abandon** → the owner presses a "close without saving" action → `:abandoned`. No `MealPlan`.
    - **No transfer:** only the `owner_user_id` can confirm or abandon. If they walk away, the session expires at 24h.
*   **`Message` (deprecated as a top-level concept):** what was originally called `Message` in the v1 CONTEXT is now `PlanningMessage` scoped to a `PlanningSession`. There is no "Account-wide chat history" — once a plan is confirmed, the conversation is closed. If the user wants to adjust the plan later, they open a **new** `PlanningSession` (see `MealPlan` lifecycle below).
*   **`Ingredient` (catalog, system-wide):** A purchasable item with name, category, default unit (`:milliliters` | `:grams` | `:units`), and one or more **`variants`** keyed by processing (e.g. `leche.uht`, `leche.organica`, `leche.comun`). Each variant has a **`conservative_shelf_life_days`** value (set by the MyFood team at catalog-create time).
*   **`InventoryItem` (per-Account):** A physical quantity of one ingredient in one account. Carries `ingredient_id`, `variant_processing`, `quantity_milli`, `purchased_at`. `estimated_expires_at` is **computed** as `purchased_at + variant.conservative_shelf_life_days`. `effective_expires_at` is `COALESCE(expires_at_override, estimated_expires_at)`. `expires_at_override` is set by the user via the UI badge tap (optional).
*   **Catalog shelf-life policy (decided grill 2026-06-16, "catastrophist"):** `conservative_shelf_life_days` is set to the **lower quartile / `floor(typical * 0.7)`** of the real distribution, never the median or mean. Rationale: the system must **never** let an item expire earlier than the announced date. If the announced date passes and the item is still good, the user can override. Inverse errors (announcing too late) are unacceptable because the AI prioritizes consumption of items with imminent `effective_expires_at`.
*   **`Recipe` (per-Account, owned by the Account):** A cookable dish. Carries `name`, `description`, `steps[]`, `servings`, `cooking_time_minutes`, `ingredients[]` (each with `ingredient_id`, `variant_processing`, `quantity_milli`). A recipe has `created_by_user_id` (the User who created it — for attribution) and `source` (`:global_catalog` | `:user_created` | `:ai_generated`). The recipe **belongs to the Account**, not to the User — if the creator leaves, the recipe stays. Any `Account` member may **read, edit, or delete** any of the Account's recipes (collaborative, decided grill 2026-06-16). The **global catalog** is a read-only library maintained by the MyFood team; copying a catalog recipe into an Account creates a user-owned clone that can be edited. AI-generated recipes are stored with `source = :ai_generated` and `created_by_user_id` set to the User who prompted them; they live only in that Account's library.
*   **`Favorite` (per-User, per-Recipe):** A User may mark a `Recipe` (not a `Meal`) as favorite. Stored as `(user_id, recipe_id)` in a join table. The planning service receives `favorite_recipe_ids = UNION(favorites per Account member)` as part of the LLM prompt context, so a recipe favored by any member is prioritized in plan proposals. The favorite is **per-User**, not per-Account: Ana and Juan each have their own list, and the AI sees the union (decided grill 2026-06-16).
*   **`Meal` (per-Account):** A scheduled instance of a `Recipe` on a date and slot. Belongs to a `MealPlan`. Has `recipe_id`, `account_id`, `date`, `slot` (`:breakfast` | `:lunch` | `:dinner`), `servings` (the actual servings for this meal), `is_cooked` (boolean), `cooked_by_user_id` (nullable, set when is_cooked becomes true), `cooked_at`. The `Meal.ingredients[]` are derived from `Recipe.ingredients[]` scaled by the meal's `servings`.
*   **`MealPlan` (per-Account):** A confirmed range of meals. Carries `account_id`, `date_from`, `date_to`, `confirmed_at`, `confirmed_by_user_id`, `status` (`:active` | `:superseded` | `:archived`), `superseded_by_plan_id` (nullable, points to the plan that replaced this one). A plan becomes `:superseded` when the owner regenerates the future portion.
*   **`ShoppingItem` (per-Account):** A "to-buy" item derived from the active `MealPlan`. Carries `account_id`, `ingredient_id`, `variant_processing`, `quantity_milli` (the **net** amount after subtracting available inventory), `from_meal_ids[]` (the meals in the active plan that require this), `status` (`:pending` | `:in_cart` | `:purchased`), `assigned_supermarket_id` (nullable, set in Vista 4), `for_date_from`, `for_date_to`.

## 4b. Account & User Deletion (GDPR-clean, decided grill 2026-06-16)

### Account deletion (A1)

When an owner deletes their `Account`:

1. **30-day grace period**: the account is soft-deleted (status `:tombstoned`), all data is hidden from queries, but recoverable via "undo" within 30 days. After 30 days, **hard-delete** is irreversible.
2. **Cascade hard-delete**: all `Recipe`s, `MealPlan`s, `Meal`s, `ShoppingItem`s, `InventoryItem`s, `Budget`, `PlanningMessage`s, and `AccountMembership`s belonging to the Account are deleted.
3. **Members are notified** (push + email): "La familia 'X' se disolvió. Si tenías datos en esa familia, exportalos antes de [fecha de hard-delete]."
4. **Other Accounts the ex-owner belonged to are untouched** (multi-familia isolation). The ex-owner's `User` keeps existing and its other memberships remain active.
5. **Recipes the ex-owner created in OTHER Accounts (where they were not the owner) survive** in those Accounts with `created_by_user_id` unchanged — those Accounts are not affected.

### User deletion (B1)

When a `User` deletes their account (full erase):

1. **All `AccountMembership`s of that User across all Accounts are deleted in cascade** (multi-familia cascade: one User may belong to N Accounts).
2. **For each Account where the User was the `:owner`**: the Account enters the A1 deletion flow (30-day grace, then hard-delete).
3. **For each Account where the User was a `:member`**: the Account loses the member. The owner of that Account is notified: "[User] se fue de tu familia."
4. **Recipes created by the User in Accounts where they were NOT the owner**: survive with `created_by_user_id = null` (anonymized attribution). The Account does not lose the recipe content.
5. **PlanningMessages from any PlanningSession the User participated in** (as `owner_user_id`): deleted with their `PlanningSession`s when the Account is hard-deleted; for Accounts that survive (User was a member, not the operator of that session), the messages already scoped to those sessions are unaffected because the User wasn't the operator.

### Data portability (x)

Every User has a **"Export my data"** action that produces a single JSON file containing:

- Profile (name, email, account_id_history, preferences).
- Recipes created by this User across all Accounts (current state).
- PlanningMessages authored by this User as `owner_user_id` (across all sessions).
- Membership history (with timestamps and statuses).

The export is generated server-side on demand, streamed as a download, and the request is logged for audit. Generated in the User's locale.

## 5. Cross-Vista Consistency Model

The five views are tightly coupled. These rules govern how state changes propagate across `MealPlan`, `ShoppingList`, and `Inventory`.

### 5.1 MealPlan regeneration lifecycle (decided grill 2026-06-16)

When the owner regenerates part of a confirmed plan:

1. **Scope is "future days only, never cooked"** — the regeneration `date_from` must be ≥ the earliest future day. Already-cooked `Meal`s (is_cooked = true) and their dates are immutable.
2. **Original `MealPlan.status` becomes `:superseded`**, `superseded_by_plan_id` points to the new plan. The cooked meals stay on the old plan as historical record.
3. **A new `PlanningSession` is opened** to drive the regeneration (no in-place edit of the previous chat).
4. **New `Meal`s** are created in the new plan for the regeneration range. `Meal.id` is new — there is no "edit in place".

### 5.2 ShoppingItem regeneration (decided grill 2026-06-16)

When a `MealPlan` is regenerated (or initially confirmed):

1. **All `ShoppingItem`s for the regenerated date range that are not yet `:purchased` are hard-deleted.**
2. **`ShoppingItem`s already `:purchased` are not touched** — they have already triggered Inventory insertion (Trigger 1) and represent committed history.
3. **New `ShoppingItem`s are computed**: for each ingredient required by the new plan in the range, `needed = Σ(meal.ingredient_quantity_milli)`, `to_buy = max(0, needed - available_inventory)` where `available_inventory = inventory.quantity_milli - Σ(reservations from non-cooked future meals)`. Persist only the `to_buy` quantity.

### 5.3 Inventory reservation model (decided grill 2026-06-16)

The "free" quantity of an `InventoryItem` is not its raw `quantity_milli` — it is reduced by reservations from future non-cooked `Meal`s.

```
free(item) = item.quantity_milli
           - Σ reservation(item, m) for each future m in the active plan
                                         where m.uses(item.ingredient_id, item.variant)
                                         and m.is_cooked == false
reservation(item, m) = m.required_quantity_milli
```

**When a `Meal` is marked cooked (Trigger 2):**

1. If the user confirms the "descontar ingredientes" prompt → `item.quantity_milli -= meal.required_quantity_milli` for each ingredient. The reservation disappears.
2. If the user rejects → the `Meal.is_cooked` is still set to true (it's a fact of life), but the Inventory is **not** modified. The reservation also disappears (the meal is no longer future). This may leave Inventory misaligned with reality; the next regeneration or manual adjustment reconciles.

**FIFO at consumption:** when multiple `InventoryItem`s exist for the same ingredient+variant, the one with the **earliest `effective_expires_at`** is consumed first (catastrophist policy).

### 5.4 Reactive recomputation

Every state change that affects ShoppingList triggers an **immediate** recompute:

- `Meal` marked cooked → recompute `free(inventory_item)` for the meal's ingredients → cascade to ShoppingItem creation/deletion/update.
- `MealPlan` regenerated → ShoppingItem hard-delete + recompute per 5.2.
- Manual Inventory adjustment (Trigger 3) → recompute reservations → cascade to ShoppingItem.
- New `InventoryItem` from purchase (Trigger 1) → recompute free → cascade to ShoppingItem (typically a deletion or quantity reduction).

There is **no batch / cron / scheduled recompute**. The UI of Vista 4 updates in real time as these events arrive via Phoenix Channels.

### 5.5 Orphan inventory (decided grill 2026-06-16)

When a `Meal` is removed by regeneration, its previously-purchased `InventoryItem`s are **not deleted**. They become **orphans**: still physical, still with `effective_expires_at`, but no longer reserved by any future meal. They are immediately available for re-assignment by the AI in any future plan (zero-waste priority).

Orphans are surfaced in Vista 5 with a "sin comida asignada" badge to nudge the user toward using them.

### 5.6 AI conflict detection (decided grill 2026-06-16)

Before the LLM finalizes a `MealPlan` proposal, the planning service checks each candidate `Meal` for **inventory feasibility**: `required ≤ free(item)` for each ingredient. If any `Meal` would be infeasible given current Inventory and reservations, the LLM is re-prompted with the conflict and asked to propose an alternative (different recipe, scaled-down quantity, or skip the day). The user only sees a plan that the system can actually execute.

## 6. AI Tone and Personality (For LLM System Prompts)
When developing the system prompts for external LLM integration (OpenAI/Gemini), the MyFood Assistant must act as a **"Personal Economist Chef"**:
*   **Tone:** Friendly, professional, warm, and highly efficient.
*   **Personality:** A blend of a nutritionist, an accountant, and an expert personal shopper who knows all the local supermarket prices.
*   **Response Objective:** Get straight to the point, maximize financial savings, minimize cooking time, and guarantee 0% food waste.
